History of Venezuela

As a component of the Republic of Colombia, Venezuela successfully gained its independence from Spain by 1819. The Colombian federation was recognised by the US in 1822. The United States recognised Venezuela in 1835 and opened diplomatic ties with it after it declared its independence from Colombia in 1830. Here is a timeline of Venezuela:

1498-99 - Christopher Columbus and Alonso de Ojeda visit Venezuela, which is inhabited by Carib, Arawak and Chibcha peoples.

1521 - Spanish colonisation begins.

1749 - First rebellion against Spanish colonial rule.

1810 - Venezuelans take advantage of Napoleon's invasion of Spain to declare independence.

1829-30 - Venezuela secedes from Gran Colombia.

1870-88 - Ruler Antonio Guzman Blanco attracts foreign investment, modernises infrastructure and develops agriculture and education.

1908-35 - Under dictator Juan Vicente Gomez, Venezuela becomes world's largest oil exporter.

1945 - Coup establishes civilian government after decades of military rule.

1948 - President Romulo Gallegos, Venezuela's first democratically-elected leader, overthrown within eight months in military coup led by Marcos Perez Jimenez.

 

The Democratic Rule

1958 - Admiral Wolfgang Larrazabal ousts Marcos Perez Jimenez; leftist Romulo Betancourt of the Democratic Action Party (AD) wins presidential election.

1973 - Venezuela benefits from oil boom and its currency peaks against the US dollar; oil and steel industries nationalised.

1989 - Carlos Andres Perez (AD) elected president amid economic depression, launches austerity programme with IMF loan. Riots, martial law and general strike follow, with hundreds killed in street violence.

1992 - Colonel Hugo Chavez and supporters make two coup attempts. Some 120 people killed in suppression of coups, Col Chavez jailed for two years before being pardoned.

1993-95 - President Perez impeached on corruption charges.

 

Rise of Chavez

1998 -  Amidst disillusionment with mainstream parties, Hugo Chavez wins the presidency and ushers in the "Bolivarian Revolution," bringing with it a new constitution, socialist and populist economic and social programmes fueled by rising oil prices, as well as a foreign policy that is becoming more and more antagonistic to the United States.

2001 - President Chavez passes measures redistributing wealth and land via the use of an enabling statute. Business and certain union groups are becoming increasingly concerned that he is attempting to consolidate political and economic power.

 2002 April - Following the nomination of a new board, armed forces mutiny against the government amid a violent standoff with the national oil monopoly.
President Chavez is placed in military prison, but Pedro Carmona's provisional administration falls apart and Hugo Chavez takes back power.

 2005 January - To end big estates in Venezuela and help the impoverished in rural areas, President Chavez announces a decree on land reform. Ranchers claim that the action is an assault on their land.

 2005 March - Slandering prominent people might result in harsh fines and jail sentences under new media restrictions.

2005 December - Parties loyal to President Chavez dominate parliament after opposition parties boycott election.

 

Russian Alliance

2006 July - President Chavez inks a $3 billion (£1.6 billion) arms deal with Russia in July 2006, signalling a shift away from US arms imports. The contract includes a purchase of fighter planes and helicopters.

2006 December - With 63% of the vote, Hugo Chavez wins a third term as president, the biggest margin since the election of 1947.

2007 January - President Chavez says  that major energy and telecommunications businesses would be nationalised in accordance with an 18-month enabling measure that has been adopted by parliament.

2007 June - Two of the biggest US oil firms, Exxon Mobil and ConocoPhilips, refuse to give the Venezuelan government overwhelming control over their activities in the Orinoco Belt, resulting in their expropriation.

2007 December - A referendum that would have expanded government control over the Central Bank and extended its authority to expropriate property was narrowly rejected, handing President Chavez his first electoral setback.

2008 September - Venezuela and Russia sign an agreement to cooperate on gas and oil. During joint drills in November, Russian warships will make their first trip back to the Americas since the Cold War, while Russian jets will be stopping over in Venezuela.

2008 October - First Venezuelan telecommunications satellite launched from China.

2009 February - Plans to eliminate term limits on elected officials' tenure in office were approved by voters in a referendum. When President Chavez's tenure ends in 2012, this enables him to take office again.

 

Economic Problems

2010 January - President Chavez devalues the bolivar currency to boost revenue from oil exports after economy shrank 5.8% in last quarter of 2009.

2010 September - Parliamentary elections. Opposition makes significant gains.

2012 April - In an effort to combat inflation, the government extended price limits on a wider range of necessities. President Chavez has threatened to seize businesses that don't follow the pricing restrictions.

2012 July - After six years, Venezuela finally joins Mercosur as a full member. The country has four years to abide by the Mercosur trading rules.

2012 October - President Chavez wins a fourth term in office.

President Chaves Dies

2013 April - Following a fight with cancer, President Hugo Chavez passes away in March at the age of 58. His selected replacement, Nicolas Maduro, wins the presidency with a slim majority. The opposition challenges the outcome.

2014 February-March - At least 28 people die in suppression of anti-government protests.

2014 November - Government announces cuts in public spending as oil prices reach a four-year low.

2015 December - Opposition Democratic Unity coalition wins two-thirds majority in parliamentary elections, ending 16 years of Socialist Party control.

2016 September -  Hundreds of thousands of protesters demonstrate in Caracas, Venezuela, demanding the overthrow of President Maduro and holding him accountable for the country's economic catastrophe.

2017 July - A contentious constituent assembly was chosen despite international censure and a boycott by the opposition.

2018 May - The proclaimed win of President Maduro in the presidential election is contested by the opposition.

2018 August - According to the UN, two million Venezuelans have fled their country to one of its neighbours since 2014.

2019 January–February - Juan Guaidó, the leader of the opposition, proclaims himself the acting president and calls on the military to remove President Maduro on the grounds that the results of the 2018 election were manipulated.
The majority of Latin American nations, the US, and the European Union all acknowledge Mr. Guaidó.

2020 December - The opposition abstains from the parliamentary elections, which President Maduro's party and allies officially win.
